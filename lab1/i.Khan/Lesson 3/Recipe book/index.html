Slow Cooker BBQ Beef Brisket

  • Cook Time: 8-10 hours
  • Serves: 4-5 servings

RUB:

INGREDIENTS QUANTITY
Beef Brisket 2 Pounds
Olive Oil 1 tbsp
Brown Sugar 1 tbsp
Paprika Powder 2 tsp
Garlic Powder 1 tsp
Onion Powder 1 tsp
Cumin Powder 1/2 tsp
Mustard Powder 3/4 tsp
Salt 1 tsp
Black Pepper 1/2 tsp

BBQ SAUCE:

INGREDIENTS QUANTITY
Garlic (minced) 2 Cloves
Apple Cider Vinegar 1/2 Cup
Ketchup 1 1/2 Cups
Brown Sugar (packed) 1/2 Cup
Black Pepper 2 tsp
Onion Powder 2 tsp
Mustard Powder 2 tsp
Cayenne Pepper 1 tsp
Worcestershire Sauce 1 tbsp

Instructions:

1. Mix RUB ingredients. Rub over the entire brisket. If time permits, leave for 30 minutes - 24 hours in the refrigerator.

2. Combine BBQ Sauce ingredients in a slow cooker. Mix, then add the brisket.

3. Turn the slow cooker on "low" and set timer for 8-10 hours.

4. Remove the brisket and place on a tray.

5. Pour the remaining liquid from the slow cooker into a saucepan. Bring it to a simmer over medium-high heat and reduce until it thickens into a syrup-like consistency.

6. Meanwhile, drizzle the brisket with oil, then roast in a 200C/390F oven for 15 minutes until brown spots appear. Remove from the oven and baste generously with the BBQ Sauce from "Step 5", then return to oven for 5 minutes. Remove and baste again, then return to the oven for 5 – 10 minutes until the surface caramelises.

7. TO SERVE: Slice brisket thinly across the grain and serve with the remaining BBQ Sauce.

Cod Ceviche

  • Total Time: 50 minutes

    • *20 mins prep / 30 mins cook

  • Serves: 4
INGREDIENTS QUANTITY
Cod (pre-frozen) 1 pound
Limes 3
Lemons 2
Grapefruit 1
Salt To Taste
Black Pepper To Taste
Red Onion 1/2 (Sliced root to tip)
Roma Tomato 2 (Seeded)
Corn 1 ear (kernels sliced off)
Habanero/Rocoto Pepper 1 (or more to taste)
Cilantro 3 tbsp chopped

Instructions:

1. Slice the fish into small, bite-sized pieces.

2. Cut the tomatoes into pieces the same size as the fish and set them aside for later.

3. Zest 1 lime, 1 lemon, and the grapefruit: grate them to a "fine" consistency.

4. Mince the habanero fine.

5. Juice all the citrus.

6. Add all the ingredients except for the tomatoes and cilantro to a bowl or plastic container with a lid, and refrigerate for 30 minutes to 2 hours.

7. Add the tomatoes and cilantro, mix well and serve cold with chips.

Notes:

Marinating times make a difference when you are making ceviche. Depending on the size of the fish pieces, you will need at least 30 minutes and normally an hour for the citrus to "cook" the fish. Two hours is fine, but beyond that the ceviche, while still good, becomes more of a pickeled fish thing.

Citrus matters, too. You always want the dominant citrus in the marinade to be either limes or lemons, which are far more acidic than oranges, grapefruits or tangerines.

Slow Cooker Lamb, Apricot and Olive Tagine

  • Total Time: 5 Hours 30 minutes

    • *30 mins prep / 5 hours cook

  • Serves: 4
INGREDIENTS QUANTITY
Lamb Shoulder (trimmed) 1 1/2 pounds (cut into 1" pieces)
Carrots 4 (cut into 1" pieces)
Onion (medium) 1 (chopped)
Dried Apricots 1/2 cup (halved)
Green Olives (pitted) 1/2 cup
Garlic 2 Cloves (chopped)
All Purpose Flour 2 tbsp
Paprika 1 tsp
Cumin (ground) 1 tsp
Ginger (ground) 1/2 tsp
Cinnamon (ground) 1/2 tsp
Kosher Salt 1 tsp
Black Pepper 1/4 tsp
Couscous 1 cup
Chopped Pistachios "For Serving"
Fresh Cilantro Leaves "For Serving"
Lemon Wedges "For Serving"

Instructions:

1. In a 4 to 6 quart slow cooker, mix together the lamb, carrots, onion, apricots, olives, garlic, flour, paprika, cumin, cinnamon, ginger, 1 teaspoon salt, ¼ teaspoon pepper, and ½ cup water.

2. Cover and cook until the lamb and vegetables are tender, on low for 8 hours or on high for 5 hours.

3. Ten minutes before serving, cook the couscous according to the package directions. Serve the lamb over the couscous with the pistachios, cilantro, and lemon wedges.

Notes:

Simple and to the point. Get cooking!
